IN the new garden, in all the parts, In cities now, modern, I wander, Though the second or third result, or still further, 
 primitive yet, Days, places, indifferent—though various, the same, Time, Paradise, the Mannahatta, the prairies, finding  
 me unchanged, Death indifferent—Is it that I lived long since? 
 Was I buried very long ago?   [ begin page 313 ]ppp.01500.321.jpg For all that, I may now be watching you here, this  
 moment; For the future, with determined will, I seek—the  
 woman of the future, You, born years, centuries after me, I seek.
