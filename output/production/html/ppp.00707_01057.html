OLD AGE'S LAMBENT PEAKS.

The touch of flame—the illuminating fire—the loftiest look at  
 last, O'er city, passion, sea—o'er prairie, mountain, wood—the earth  
 itself; The airy, different, changing hues of all, in falling twilight, Objects and groups, bearings, faces, reminiscences; The calmer sight—the golden setting, clear and broad: So much i' the atmosphere, the points of view, the situations  
 whence we scan, Bro't out by them alone—so much (perhaps the best) unreck'd  
 before; The lights indeed from them—old age's lambent peaks.
