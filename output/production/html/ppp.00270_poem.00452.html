AS THE TIME DRAWS NIGH.

1

As the time draws nigh, glooming, a cloud, A dread beyond, of I know not what, darkens me. I shall go forth, I shall traverse The States awhile—but I cannot tell  
 whither or how long; Perhaps soon, some day or night while I am singing,  
 my voice will suddenly cease.

2

O book, O chants! must all then amount to but this? Must we barely arrive at this beginning of us?…  
 And yet it is enough, O soul! O soul! we have positively appear'd—that is enough.
