OUT OF THE ROLLING OCEAN, THE 
  CROWD.

1

OUT of the rolling ocean, the crowd, came a drop gently  
 to me, Whispering, I love you, before long I die, I have travel'd a long way, merely to look on you, to touch you, For I could not die till I once look'd on you, For I fear'd I might afterward lose you.

2

(Now we have met, we have look'd, we are safe; Return in peace to the ocean my love; I too am part of that ocean, my love—we are not so  
 much separated; Behold the great rondure—the cohesion of all, how per- 
 fect! But as for me, for you, the irresistible sea is to separ- 
 ate us, As for an hour carrying us diverse—yet cannot carry  
 us diverse for ever; Be not impatient—a little space—know you, I salute  
 the air, the ocean and the land, Every day, at sundown, for your dear sake, my love.)
