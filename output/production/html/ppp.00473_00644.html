LONG, TOO LONG, O LAND.

LONG, too long, O land, Traveling roads all even and peaceful, you learn'd from  
 joys and prosperity only; But now, ah now, to learn from crises of anguish—ad- 
 vancing, grappling with direst fate, and recoiling  
 not; And now to conceive, and show to the world, what your  
 children en-masse really are; (For who except myself has yet conceived what your  
 children en-masse really are?)
