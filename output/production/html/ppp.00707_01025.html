WASHINGTON'S MONUMENT, FEBRUARY, 1885.

Ah, not this marble, dead and cold: Far from its base and shaft expanding—the round zones circling,  
 comprehending, Thou, Washington, art all the world's, the continents' entire— 
 not yours alone, America, Europe's as well, in every part, castle of lord or laborer's cot, Or frozen North, or sultry South—the African's—the Arab's in  
 his tent, Old Asia's there with venerable smile, seated amid her ruins; (Greets the antique the hero new? 'tis but the same—the heir  
 legitimate, continued ever, The indomitable heart and arm—proofs of the never-broken  
 line, Courage, alertness, patience, faith, the same—e'en in defeat de- 
 feated not, the same:) Wherever sails a ship, or house is built on land, or day or night, Through teeming cities' streets, indoors or out, factories or farms, Now, or to come, or past—where patriot wills existed or exist, Wherever Freedom, pois'd by Toleration, sway'd by Law, Stands or is rising thy true monument.
