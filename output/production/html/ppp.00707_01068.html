TO THE PENDING YEAR.

HAVE I no weapon-word for thee—some message brief and  
 fierce? (Have I fought out and done indeed the battle?) Is there no shot  
 left, For all thy affectations, lisps, scorns, manifold silliness? Nor for myself—my own rebellious self in thee? Down, down, proud gorge!—though choking thee; Thy bearded throat and high-borne forehead to the gutter; Crouch low thy neck to eleemosynary gifts.
