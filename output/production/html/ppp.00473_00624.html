CAVALRY CROSSING A FORD.

A LINE in long array, where they wind betwixt green  
 islands; They take a serpentine course—their arms flash in the  
 sun—Hark to the musical clank; Behold the silvery river—in it the splashing horses,  
 loitering, stop to drink; Behold the brown-faced men—each group, each person,  
 a picture—the negligent rest on the saddles; Some emerge on the opposite bank—others are just  
 entering the ford; The guidon flags flutter gaily in the wind.
