TO RICH GIVERS.

WHAT you give me I cheerfully accept, A little sustenance, a hut and garden, a little money, as I rendez- 
 vous with my poems, A traveler's lodging and breakfast as I journey through the States,  
 —why should I be ashamed to own such gifts? why to  
 advertise for them? For I myself am not one who bestows nothing upon man and woman, For I bestow upon any man or woman the entrance to all the gifts  
 of the universe.
