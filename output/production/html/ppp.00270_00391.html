An Army Corps on the March.

WITH its cloud of skirmishers in advance, With now the sound of a single shot, snapping like a  
 whip, and now an irregular volley, The swarming ranks press on and on, the dense brigades  
 press on; Glittering dimly, toiling under the sun—the dust-cover'd  
 men, In columns rise and fall to the undulations of the  
 ground, With artillery interspers'd—the wheels rumble, the  
 horses sweat, As the army corps advances.
